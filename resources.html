Resources: Your Toolkit for the Journey

Navigating the U.S. application process from Bangladesh requires leveraging all available tools and information. This page compiles helpful resources, many mentioned throughout the guide, into one place. Remember to always cross-reference information and prioritize official sources (college websites, testing agencies) when possible.

Official Testing & Application Platforms:

  • College Board: collegeboard.org (SAT, AP Exams, CSS Profile)
  • ACT: act.org
  • TOEFL: ets.org/toefl
  • IELTS: ielts.org (Check for local test centers via British Council or IDP)
  • Duolingo English Test: englishtest.duolingo.com
  • Common Application: commonapp.org
  • Scoir: scoir.com
  • ApplyTexas: goapplytexas.org
  • University of California Application: apply.universityofcalifornia.edu

College Search & Research Tools:

  • College Websites: The primary source for admission requirements, financial aid policies (look for international student sections!), academic programs, and deadlines.
  • Common Data Set (CDS): Google "[College Name] Common Data Set". Provides detailed institutional data, including financial aid statistics for non-resident aliens (Section H6). Can be dense but very informative.
  • BigFuture (College Board): bigfuture.collegeboard.org (General college search tool, though aid info for internationals might be limited).
  • Niche: niche.com (Student reviews, rankings, search filters - take rankings/reviews with a grain of salt).
  • College Navigator (US Dept of Ed): nces.ed.gov/collegenavigator/ (Official government data, less user-friendly but reliable stats).

Financial Aid Resources:

  • College Financial Aid Websites: Crucial for understanding specific policies for international students, required forms (CSS/ISFAA/Other), and deadlines.
  • CSS Profile: cssprofile.collegeboard.org
  • ISFAA Form: Usually downloadable directly from the financial aid websites of colleges that use it.
  • FinAid.org: finaid.org (General resource, though heavily focused on US domestic students, some info might be useful).
  • EducationUSA: educationusa.state.gov (U.S. State Department network offering advising services, sometimes including info on funding. Check for centers/advisors covering Bangladesh).

Online Communities & Forums (Use Critically!):

  • Reddit:
    • r/ApplyingToCollege: General college admissions discussion. Use the search function!
    • r/IntltoUSA: Specifically for international students applying to the US. High traffic, good source of peer experiences and advice (but verify info).
    • r/SAT & r/ACT: Test prep discussions and resources.
  • College Confidential: collegeconfidential.com (Long-standing forum, can be helpful but sometimes prone to misinformation or stress-inducing content. Use search cautiously).

Test Preparation Resources:

  • Khan Academy: khanacademy.org (Official FREE digital SAT prep partner).
  • Official ACT Prep Guide: Available for purchase, contains official practice tests.
  • Official TOEFL/IELTS/Duolingo Practice Materials: Available on their respective websites (some free, some paid).
  • Reputable Prep Books: Barron's, Kaplan, Princeton Review (ensure they are updated for current test formats, especially the Digital SAT).

Bangladeshi Context & Support (Explore Locally):

  • School Counselors/Teachers: Your first point of contact for transcripts, recommendations, and potentially fee waiver verification.
  • EducationUSA Centers/Advisors in Bangladesh: Check the EdUSA website for local contacts who may offer workshops or guidance.
  • Local Alumni Networks: Connect with current students or recent alumni from Bangladesh studying in the U.S. (via LinkedIn, social media groups) for firsthand insights.
  • Reputable Local College Counseling Services: (Vet carefully! Check credentials, success rates, and ethical practices. Be wary of exorbitant fees or guaranteed admission promises).

Disclaimer: This list is not exhaustive, and website links/policies can change. Always verify information directly with the relevant institution or organization.

Glossary of Key Terms

Need-Blind Admission
The policy where a university evaluates applications and makes admission decisions without considering an applicant's financial situation or ability to pay.
Need-Aware Admission
The policy where a university *may* consider an applicant's ability to pay as part of the admission decision process, often for borderline applicants or to manage its financial aid budget.
CSS Profile
An online application (managed by College Board) used by many private colleges and scholarship programs to determine eligibility for their own institutional financial aid funds (distinct from federal aid).
ISFAA (International Student Financial Aid Application)
A paper or PDF form used by some colleges (especially those not using the CSS Profile) to collect financial information from international students applying for financial aid.
Common Application (Common App)
A standardized undergraduate college application platform accepted by over 1,000 member colleges and universities.
FAFSA (Free Application for Federal Student Aid)
The form used to apply for US federal student aid (grants, loans, work-study). International students are generally *not* eligible for federal aid and typically do not fill out the FAFSA, unless they are dual citizens or eligible non-citizens.
Early Decision (ED)
A binding early application plan. If you apply ED and are accepted, you are generally obligated to enroll at that university and withdraw all other applications.
Early Action (EA)
A non-binding early application plan. You apply early and receive a decision early, but you are not obligated to enroll if accepted and can compare offers from other colleges.
Regular Decision (RD)
The standard application deadline (usually in January or February) and decision timeline (usually in March or April).
TOEFL / IELTS / DET
Standardized tests designed to assess the English language proficiency of non-native speakers. Required by most US universities for international applicants whose primary language of instruction was not English.
Demonstrated Interest
Actions taken by a student that show genuine interest in a particular college (e.g., visiting campus, attending virtual info sessions, contacting admissions). Some colleges consider this in admissions.
Waitlist
An outcome where an applicant is neither accepted nor rejected but placed on a waiting list. Admission may be offered later if space becomes available.
